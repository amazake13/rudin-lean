12 Bounded Operators on a Hilbert Space

Definition 39 Rudin 12.9, adjoint of a bounded operator
✓

For Hilbert spaces \(E\), \(F\) and \(A \in \mathcal{B}(E,F)\), there is a unique bounded operator \(A^{*} \in \mathcal{B}(F,E)\) characterised by \(\langle A x, y\rangle = \langle x, A^{*} y\rangle \) for all \(x \in E\), \(y \in F\).

A self-adjoint operator \(T\) on a finite-dimensional inner product space \(E\) is unitarily equivalent to the diagonal operator on the orthogonal direct sum of its eigenspaces: there is a linear isometry \(E \cong \bigoplus _\mu E_\mu \) conjugating \(T\) to multiplication by \(\mu \) on each summand.
